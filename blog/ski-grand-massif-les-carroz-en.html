Skiing the Grand Massif from Les Carroz: 265 km of slopes

By Lodge Le Chamois · July 08, 2026

If you're after a large linked ski area without the crowds of the mega-resorts, the Grand Massif is a safe bet. And Les Carroz d'Arâches is one of its finest gateways.

One of the largest ski areas in France

With 265 km of slopes, the Grand Massif ranks as the country's 4th-largest linked area. It brings together five resorts — Flaine, Les Carroz, Morillon, Samoëns and Sixt — with 139 runs: 13 black, 46 red, 60 blue and 20 green, served by 62 lifts. The area tops out at 2,500 m, with reliable snow cover on the Flaine heights.

Ski-in access from Les Carroz

The Carroz cable car is just a 3-minute walk from our apartment — one skibus stop away. It gives direct access to the area and the ski-in link to Flaine and the other resorts. Ski passes can be bought at the Tourist Office or online.

Our tips for reaching the slopes

  • The Kédeuze gondola: take skibus line B in front of the residence to the Bardelle stop, or walk up the sledging slope then take the Figaro carpet lift to be fully independent.
  • The Molliets chairlift: ideal if you have no children to drop at ski school. A large free car park awaits if you prefer to drive.
  • Equipment rental: Zig Zag Sports is right opposite the residence — remember to book ahead.

A heated ski locker at the residence

Practical and often overlooked: our apartment has a secure ski locker on the ground floor, fitted with a boot dryer. You store skis and boots in the warm, without cluttering the apartment, and set off with dry boots every morning.
